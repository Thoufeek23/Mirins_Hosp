Minimally Invasive Surgery

Minimally invasive surgery (MIS) refers to surgical techniques that use smaller incisions, specialized instruments, and advanced imaging technologies to perform procedures with less trauma to the body compared to traditional open surgery. The most common types of minimally invasive surgeries include laparoscopic (using a camera and small instruments inserted through small incisions), robotic-assisted surgery, and endoscopic surgery. These procedures are typically associated with shorter recovery times, less pain, reduced risk of infection, and minimal scarring. MIS is commonly used in a variety of specialties, such as gynecology, urology, orthopedics, and general surgery. Conditions like gallbladder disease, hernias, and certain cancers can be treated effectively with these techniques. Although minimally invasive surgery offers several benefits, it requires a high level of skill from the surgeon and specialized equipment, and it may not be suitable for all types of surgeries.
