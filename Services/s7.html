STDs

Sexually transmitted diseases (STDs) are infections passed from person to person through sexual contact. Common STDs include chlamydia, gonorrhea, syphilis, and HIV, each of which can have long-term health consequences if left untreated. Regular screenings, especially for sexually active individuals, are essential for early detection. Treatment typically involves antibiotics or antiviral medications, depending on the infection type. Preventive measures include the use of condoms, vaccination (such as for HPV), and open communication with partners about sexual health. Healthcare providers offer education, testing, and treatment to help manage and prevent the spread of STDs, promoting sexual health and well-being.
